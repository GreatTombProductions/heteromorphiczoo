## Executive Summary

**The gap is enormous.** No band has published a proactive, public-facing AI disclosure policy page on their website. Every existing artist statement is reactive (social media posts, interviews, press releases). Every industry body position is defensive-institutional. HZ publishing a permanent, findable, creatively voiced AI policy page on their website is unprecedented in music. The bar is low and the opportunity to set the template is real.

## 2. Where HZ Is Distinctive (The Contrast Frame)

Four gaps HZ fills that nobody else occupies:

### Gap 1: No artist has a website AI policy page
Every existing statement is embedded in interviews, tweets, or press releases. A permanent, findable, creatively voiced policy page is unprecedented. HZ's policy becomes the template others reference.

### Gap 2: Nobody distinguishes operations AI from creative AI
The entire discourse is binary: "AI music" vs "human music." HZ saying "we use AI for website/ops but all music/art/lyrics are 100% human" occupies a nuance gap nobody is filling. The industry bodies and artists both treat AI as monolithic.

### Gap 3: Tone gap
Everything existing is either:
- Corporate-legal (RIAA/IFPI)
- Existential alarm (NMPA)
- Casual social media (Grimes)
- Blanket rejection (Metal Archives, ROKK)

A creative statement with clear specificity — neither a legal disclaimer nor a manifesto — is a register nobody is using. The liturgical voice spec gives HZ a tone that doesn't exist in the AI policy landscape.

### Gap 4: Metal scene specifically
The metal discourse is entirely *anti-AI* (bans, outrage at clones). A band that acknowledges using AI where appropriate while drawing a hard line on creative output would be the first to treat the topic with nuance rather than blanket rejection.

## 3. Stealable Components

### 3a. Intake Form Patterns (for the AI impact contact form)

No organization has built a gold-standard public AI impact intake form. Most rely on informal email channels or petition signatures. Key patterns worth stealing:

**1. Specific behavioral triggers over vague categories.**
- WGA's model: "Were you asked to use AI or given AI-generated material?" beats "Has AI affected you?"
- **HZ version:** "Has AI-generated music replaced work you would have been hired for? Has your voice or likeness been used without consent? Has AI-generated content been falsely attributed to you?"
- Source: WGA East AI Advisory Committee contact form

**2. Minimal fields, maximum signal.**
- Name (optional), email (optional for anonymous), one category dropdown (lost work / unauthorized likeness / misattribution / other), free-text description
- Source: AI Incident Database (incidentdatabase.ai) taxonomy approach

**3. Explanation-first privacy language.**
- EU AI Act Article 85 model: explanation from deployer first, then escalation
- **HZ version:** "We read every submission. We may use anonymized, aggregated patterns in our advocacy. We will never share your name or details without explicit permission."

**4. Honest "what happens next" — no overpromise.**
- WGA model: "This helps us understand the landscape and fight for protections."
- **HZ version:** "Your submission informs our advocacy and public statements. We are not lawyers and cannot represent you, but we can amplify patterns we see and connect you with organizations that can help."

**5. Allow anonymous submission.**
- Fear of reprisal is the primary barrier to reporting algorithmic harm (Amnesty research)
- Make name and email optional. The signal is in the pattern, not the identity.

### 3b. The Generative vs. Modeling Distinction (Vocabulary)

The cleanest vocabulary already in public circulation:

| Term | Meaning | Examples | Regulatory Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Generative AI** | Systems that produce novel content (text, images, audio, music) | ChatGPT, Suno, Udio, DALL-E | EU AI Act Article 50 transparency obligations apply |
| **Modeling/Simulation AI** | Systems that digitally replicate physical system behavior | Neural DSP, Kemper, Line 6 | Not subject to generative AI transparency rules |

**How the amp modeling industry describes itself:**
- Neural DSP: "machine learning-based modeling" / "neural network amp modeling" — they lean INTO ML terminology but frame it as *capturing* existing analog behavior
- Kemper: "profiling"
- Line 6: "modeling" without AI framing at all

**The load-bearing word is "simulate."** It is universally understood, legally distinct from generation, and already how the amp modeling industry describes itself.

**Recommended distinction for the policy (adapt register):**
> "We distinguish between generative AI — tools that create novel creative content like music, lyrics, or artwork — and modeling/simulation tools — systems that digitally replicate the behavior of physical equipment, like amp and effects modeling. The former replaces the player. The latter is a tool in the hand of the player."

**ISO/IEC 22989:2022** (AI concepts and terminology) supports this as a formal distinction between generative models and simulation/digital-twin models. The EU AI Act's transparency obligations (Article 50) apply specifically to systems generating "synthetic" content — physics simulation does not trigger these obligations. The distinction is both technically precise and legally grounded.

### 3c. The "We Took the Meme Seriously" Frame

The council converged on Ray's framing: "AI was supposed to do the menial things so I could do meaningful things." This is the viral vector. Design for quotation.

**Contrast with existing stances:**
- Industry bodies say "AI shouldn't replace humans" (defensive)
- Artists say "I'll never use AI" (blanket rejection)
- Labels say "AI only with our permission and revenue share" (commercial)
- **HZ says "We used AI exactly how it was supposed to be used — for infrastructure — and kept the human part human"** (affirmative, specific, demonstrable)

This is the only stance that acknowledges AI's value while drawing a hard line. It's also the only one backed by evidence (the website itself demonstrates the infrastructure claim; the chronicle documents the human creative process).

## 4. Design Recommendations (for Rimuru + CZ)

### Policy Page Architecture
- **Separate page** (`/policy` or integrated into footer expandable), not a buried FAQ entry
- **Section-linkable URLs** — fans will share specific sections: `/policy#every-note-is-human`
- **Screenshot-survivable Section 1** — must work as a standalone image when shared on social media
- **Constitutive ceremony register** (CZ's term from council) — the statement IS the stance, not a description of it

### Contact Form Architecture (for `/sanctuary` or `/signal`)
- Separate from the menagerie signup — different purpose, different form
- Gold accent (`--accent-gold`), not magenta CTA — sacred concern, not engagement invitation
- Category dropdown with specific behavioral triggers (not "tell us your story")
- Anonymous submission permitted
- "What happens next" transparency immediately visible, not buried in fine print
- Consider linking to actual advocacy resources: SAG-AFTRA AI Advisory Committee, Authors Guild, Human Artistry Campaign
