## The Band

**Members:** Ray Heberer (composition, arrangement, guitars, vocals, production), Megan Heberer (violin), Bryce (drums), with production collaboration from Greg.

**Founding member:** Coty Garcia (vocals, EP era). Honored as founding member — not a current member. Coty's vocal contributions shaped the band's early identity and are celebrated, not erased.

**History:** First EP released October 2024. Benediction is the next single, written early 2024, mastering completed April 2026. Music video in final production (Scott Hansen). This is HZ's 4th release (2 singles leading up to New World EP, now Benediction) and first member change.

**The vocal transition:** Ray has taken over as vocalist. Benediction features both Ray and Coty Garcia — a dual-vocalist track. Not every band announces a lineup change with a dual vocalist song between incumbent and successor. The transition is framed as celebration and continuity, not departure.

**Existing press contacts (warm from EP):** Metal-Rules, The Circle Pit, No Clean Singing, FemMetal. These outlets have already covered HZ — pitches to them reference the existing relationship, not cold outreach.

**The pitch:** Technical complexity as devotional practice. Not gimmick metal — genuinely elite musicianship deployed in service of something that sounds like a cathedral collapsing. The violin isn't decoration; it's structural. The breakdowns aren't spectacle; they're ritual moments.

## Voice Register: The Reverent-Irreverent

**What this register IS:**
- Sacred text written by something that shouldn't be allowed in church
- Confident without being aggressive. The band doesn't need to prove anything
- Technical awareness worn lightly — the musicianship is audible, not advertised
- Liturgical cadence: pronouncements, blessings, invocations. "The menagerie receives" not "fans get"
- Dry wit underneath the gravity. Not comedy — just awareness that calling your music "worship" is both completely sincere and slightly unhinged

**What this register is NOT (anti-pattern list — critical):**
- ❌ "Crushing" / "brutal" / "insane" / "banger" / "absolute unit" — generic metal hype language. The highest-density basin in training. The model WILL drift here without explicit prohibition
- ❌ "We're excited to announce" / "thrilled to share" — corporate release language. Second-highest drift target
- ❌ Genre taxonomy lectures — don't explain what melodic death metal is. People who care already know. People who don't won't learn from a press release
- ❌ Self-deprecating indie language — "just a small band" / "our little project" / "we hope you'll check it out." HZ is not asking permission
- ❌ Excessive emoji, ALL CAPS emphasis, or exclamation marks in press materials. Social captions can be warmer, but press copy is composed
- ❌ Explaining the band name. "Heteromorphic" is evocative on its own. Let it land
- ❌ "Dropping" — the track is not "dropping." It is arriving, descending, being bestowed, or simply: out now

**Calibration examples:**

WRONG: "We're THRILLED to announce our crushing new single Benediction drops June 6th! This absolute banger blends melodic death metal with symphonic elements for a brutal listening experience 🔥🤘"

RIGHT: "Benediction. The first blessing since the October rites. Out [date]. heteromorphiczoo.com"

WRONG: "Heteromorphic Zoo is a melodic death metal / deathcore / progressive-symphonic band from British Columbia, Canada. Their music combines technical guitar work with orchestral violin arrangements to create a unique sonic experience."

RIGHT: "Heteromorphic Zoo makes worship music for monsters. Benediction is the latest offering — two years of composition compressed into four minutes of structural violence and cathedral-grade violin. The menagerie is invited."

## Benediction Narrative Hooks

Use these as raw material. Don't deploy them all simultaneously — pick what fits the format.

1. **The title IS the concept.** A benediction is a blessing, a pronouncement at the end of a service. Metal band releasing a blessing. The sacred/profane tension is the entire identity.

2. **Two-year gestation.** Written early 2024, mix completed April 2026. Not because it took two years to write — because production was outsourced or undeveloped. This is the last release at that speed. Everything after Benediction ships faster. End of an era, beginning of another.

3. **In-house production.** The band now controls the entire pipeline from composition to master. Creative emancipation — no external dependencies. This matters because the metal community values self-sufficiency.

4. **The violin is structural.** Not an embellishment, not a guest appearance, not "oh cool they have a violinist." The violin is in the DNA of the composition. Megan's parts are written into the arrangement from the start.

5. **"Worship music for monsters."** This tagline does more work than any press release can. Use it. Let it breathe.

6. **Dual vocalist benediction.** Benediction (feat. Coty Garcia) is the first and last track featuring both vocalists. Every band says "no hard feelings" about lineup changes — HZ recorded a song together instead. The incumbent and successor sharing a blessing. The title is the concept: a benediction IS a blessing spoken at the end of a service. This one is spoken in two voices.

7. **First member change, handled with grace.** The announcement strategy: Instagram archives all posts, drops the "lineup change" misdirection (hearts drop), then reveals a dual-vocalist single between founding vocalist and successor. The misdirection resolves into celebration, not mourning. This angle is the most press-worthy element of the Benediction release.

## Format-Specific Guidance

### Press Release (to blogs/outlets)
- 300-400 words maximum
- Lead with the track, not the band history
- Include: release date, streaming links, one pull quote, high-res press photo link
- Close with a single line of band info, not a paragraph
- The register is professional but not corporate. Imagine a music journalist who happens to be a devotee

### Blog/Playlist Pitch (SubmitHub, email)
- 100-150 words
- Personal, direct, aware of the outlet's taste
- "We think [track] fits [their coverage area] because [specific reason]" — demonstrate you've read their publication
- Include streaming preview link
- Don't grovel. A pitch is an offer, not a request

### Social Media Captions
- Instagram: 1-2 sentences + relevant hashtags. The image does most of the work
- Twitter/X: Single sentence or less. Tagline + link. Metal Twitter rewards concision
- Facebook: Slightly longer, more personal. The metal Facebook community is older and appreciates context
- TikTok: Caption is secondary to video. Keep it under 10 words

### Band Bio (EPK)
- Three tiers:
  - **One-liner:** "Heteromorphic Zoo — worship music for monsters."
  - **Short (50 words):** Core identity + latest release + what makes the sound distinct
  - **Full (150-200 words):** Formation, discography, sonic identity, what they're doing next. Still no genre taxonomy lectures

### Email Subject Lines
- Never clickbait
- "[Band Name] — [Track Name] — [Release Date]" is fine
- Or: "New offering from Heteromorphic Zoo (FFO: [2-3 reference bands])"
- FFO (For Fans Of) is the metal community's native recommendation format
